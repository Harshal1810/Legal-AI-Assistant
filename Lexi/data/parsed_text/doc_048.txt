32. Mr. Pravin Anand, learned counsel for the Plaintiffs sets up the Plaintiffs' case as under:
32.1. Plaintiff No.1, Azuga, Inc., is a California-based company specializing in GPS
fleet tracking and management services, while Plaintiff No.2, Azuga Telematics
Private Limited, is located in Bangalore, India, handling engineering and product
management for Azuga's operations in India. In September 2021, both companies
were acquired by Bridgestone Americas, Inc., a subsidiary of Bridgestone
Corporation, as part of a strategy to enhance their tire-centric mobility solutions,
focusing on improving fleet safety, performance, and sustainability.
32.2. The Plaintiffs offer comprehensive fleet solutions for various sectors,
integrating hardware technology and data analytics to enhance fleet management,
safety, and efficiency for over 13,000 commercial fleets. Their services include GPS
vehicle tracking, driver monitoring, and route optimization, receiving multiple
awards for innovation and excellence.
Additionally, their website, https://www.azuga.com, provides detailed information about their
offerings, accessible to a global audience, including in India.

at paragraph 8 of the plaint. Plaintiff No.1 has also obtained registrations and filed applications for
the trademarks 'AZUGA' in the United States, Germany, Mexico, and the European Union, with the
earliest registration dating back to 23.10.2012.

35. This Court has heard the learned counsel for the parties and has perused the record.
36. The Plaintiffs are the registered proprietor of the mark 'AZUGA', both in India and
internationally, has been using the mark 'AZUGA/ ', since 2022. Defendant no. 3 is an ex-employee
of Plaintiff no. 2 and has connection with Defendant no.1. In these facts, the Defendants adoption of
the name 'AZUGA' in the identical services as that of the Plaintiffs, prima facie appears mala fide to
the Court.
37. The Plaintiff has also demonstrated that Defendants have copied pictures from Plaintiff's website
on an 'as-is' basis using them on their own website.
38. Given the well-established recognition of the Plaintiffs' trademarks, Defendants use of a
deceptively similar mark is likely to mislead the public into believing an association with the
Plaintiffs, thereby causing confusion and diluting the distinctive character and identity of the
Plaintiffs' AZUGA brand. The Defendants have unlawfully adopted and used the Plaintiffs'
registered trademark 'AZUGA', as well as infringed the Plaintiffs' copyright in product photographs
and images, all in connection with identical services.
39. In the view of the above, the Plaintiffs have made out a prima facie case for the grant of
ad-interim injunction, balance of convenience lies in favour of the Plaintiffs and irreparable harm
will be caused to the Plaintiffs, in case the restraint is not granted.
