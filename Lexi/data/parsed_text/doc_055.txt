JUDGMENT
This appeal is directed against the impugned judgment and decree dated 15.12.2015 passed in
O.S.No.3810/2010 by the XVIII Additional City Civil Judge, Bengaluru City, whereby the suit filed
by respondent No.1/plaintiff against respondent No.2/defendant for specific performance of
lease-cum-sale agreement dated 10.10.1986 in relation to the suit schedule immovable property was
decreed by the trial Court in favour of respondent No.1/plaintiff against the respondent
No.2/defendant.
2. Heard the arguments on both sides. The material on record discloses that respondent
No.1/plaintiff instituted the aforesaid suit for specific performance and other reliefs against the
respondent No.2/defendant. In the said suit, respondent No.1/plaintiff specifically contended that
respondent No.2/Sangha had executed lease-cum-sale agreement dated 10.10.1986 and issued a
possession certificate dated 18.12.1986 and had put the plaintiff in possession and enjoyment of the
suit schedule property.
NC: 2023:KHC:41743 It was contended that despite execution of the aforesaid document,
respondent No.2-Sangha was attempting to alienate the suit schedule property in favour of third
parties and as such, respondent No.1/plaintiff had instituted the said suit for specific performance
and other reliefs.
3. The respondent No.2/defendant filed his written statement inter alia disputing and denying the
various claims and contentions urged by the plaintiff. The defendant specifically contended that
plaintiff was an utter stranger and not a member of the defendant/Sangha. It was also contended
that pursuant to formation of a layout in Sy.Nos.45 and 47 of Jakkasandra Village, Begur Hobli,
Madivala I Stage, Bengaluru South Taluk, respondent No.2/Sangha had allotted site No.543 in
favour of the appellant herein vide registered lease-cum-sale agreement dated 17.09.1987 and ever
since appellant was in possession and enjoyment of the suit schedule property. Under these
circumstances, respondent No.2/Sangha sought for dismissal of the suit.

7. A perusal of the aforesaid documents comprising of the registered lease-cum-sale agreement

8. As stated supra, the appellant herein who claimed through and under the defendant/Sangha was
not arrayed as a party to the suit. However, in the light of the specific contention of the
defendant/Sangha that it had alienated the suit schedule property in favour of the appellant herein
and on the ground that her alleged right, title, interest and possession over the suit schedule
property would be affected by the impugned judgment and decree, therefore, the appellant has
preferred the present appeal along with I.A.No.2/2018 for permission to file the appeal. While
dealing with the applications, I.A.No.5/2018 for additional documents, I have already come to the
conclusion is that the said documents are relevant and necessary for the purpose of adjudication of
the issues in controversy between the parties, particularly, in the light of the specific contention of
the appellant and the defendant/Sanga that the suit schedule property was alienated in favour of the
appellant prior to institution of the suit. Under these circumstances, I am of the view that an
opportunity be provided in favour of the appellant to prosecute the present appeal and accordingly
I.A.No.2/2018 filed by the appellant is hereby allowed.

9. I.A.No.1/2018 is filed by the appellant seeking condonation of the delay of 677 days in filing the

10. Insofar as, I.A.No.3/2018 is filed by the appellant seeking impleadment as an additional
defendant to the suit is concerned, as stated supra, despite the appellant having obtained NC:
2023:KHC:41743 registered lease-cum-sale agreement on 17.09.1987 prior to the institution of the

schedule property, which was subject matter of the suit and which culminated in the impugned
judgment and decree. While it is true that in a suit for specific performance, a non-party to the
contract would ordinarily not be construed or treated as a proper and necessary party, in the light of
the specific assertion on the part of the Sangha in the written statement, that the suit was liable to be
dismissed in view of execution of registered lease-cum- sale agreement dated 17.09.1987 in favour of
the appellant, prior to the institution of the suit and since defendant/Sangha did not exercise due
diligence and did not cross-examine PW.1 or adduced any oral or documentary evidence, on his
behalf, it would be necessary for the appellant to take suitable steps to safe guard her alleged right,
title, interest and possession of the suit schedule property by getting herself impleaded as additional
defendant in the suit. Under these circumstances, I am of the view that in the peculiar/special facts
and circumstances of the instant case, the appellant is both proper and necessary party to the suit
and is

11. The next question that arise for consideration is the procedure to be followed for the purpose of
disposal of the appeal as contemplated under Order 41 Rule 28 of the Civil Procedure Code, 1908.
While dealing with the applications, I.A.No.1/2018, 2/2018, 3/2018 and 5/2018, I have already
come to the conclusion that the appellant is both proper and necessary party to the suit and
documents produced along with I.A.No.5/2018 are relevant and necessary for the purpose of
adjudication of the issues in controversy between the parties. As stated earlier, the
defendant/Sangha neither cross examined PW.1 nor adduced any oral or documentary evidence on
his behalf. Further upon impleadment, an opportunity would have to be provided in favour of the
appellant to file her written statement as defendant No.2 and contest the suit on merits. Under these
circumstances, I am of the view that the impugned judgment and decree passed by the trial Court
deserves to set aside and the matter be remitted back to the
