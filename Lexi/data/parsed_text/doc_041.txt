JUDGMENT
The Petitioners No.1 to 4 have filed the present petition as against the Respondents No.1 and 2
under Section 166 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1989, praying to award compensation of Rupees
40,00,000/- with interest at the rate of 12% p.a., in respect of death of Sri. M. Eshwarappa S/o.
Venkataramanappa.
2. The brief averments of the Petitioners' case are as follows;
a) On 01.04.2015 at about 10.15 p.m., the deceased was walking on extreme side of Bangalore Main
Road, near Chandu Motors, Singasandra. At that time, a Bus bearing Registration No.KA-51-C-114
driven by its driver from Hosa Road to proceed towards Kudlu Gate in a high speed, in a rash and
negligent manner, without observing traffic rules, dashed to the deceased. Due to the impact, the
deceased fell down and sustained grievous injuries.
b) Immediately after the accident, the deceased was shifted to LIVE 100 Hospital for treatment. The
Doctor in LIVE 100 Hospital examined and declared that, the deceased succumbed on the way to
the Hospital. Thereafter, post mortem was conducted at Victoria Hospital and the body of the
deceased was handed over to them to perform his last rights. They have spent Rupees 1,00,000/- for
funeral and obsequies ceremonies.
c) As on the date of accident, the deceased was hale and healthy and he was aged about 32 years and
he was working as a Security Guard at M/s. ISS SDB Security Services Private Limited, Bangalore
and getting salary of Rupees 12,000/- per month.
d) The only bread earner of the family has taken away. This accident occurred due to rash and

27. As this Tribunal has already observed and come to the conclusion based on the contents of
Ex.P.10 Pay Slip for the month of March 2015 and Ex.P.11 Bank Statement that, at the time of
accident, the deceased was not a self employee and he was not under fixed wages. While discussing

was 32 years old, i.e., below 40 years. Under such circumstances, the Petitioners are entitled for
future prospectus in view of death of deceased.
28. As per the decision reported in 2013 ACJ 1403 (Rajesh and Others V/s Rajbir Singh and Others)
and as the deceased was aged 32 years at the time of accident, towards future prospects 50% of the
income has to be added. So, 50% of Rupees 11,970-55 comes to Rupees 5,985-27. Therefore, the
income of the deceased comes to Rupees 17,955-82 p.m. (Rs.11,970-55 + 5,985-27).
29. The Petitioners No.1, 2 and 4, are considered as dependents of the deceased. Therefore, deceased
left behind 3 dependents. As per the principles laid down in Sarala Varma's Case, considering the
number of the dependents, i.e., 3, 1/3rd of the income has to be deducted towards personal expenses
of the deceased, i.e., Rupees 5,985-27 (1/3rd of Rs.17,955-82). Therefore, loss of dependency comes
to Rupees 11,970-55 (Rs.17,955-82/- (-) Rs.5,985-27). The multiplier corresponding to the age of the
deceased, i.e., 32 years, is 16 as per Sarala Varma's Case. Therefore, loss of dependency comes to
Rupees 22,98,345- 60 (Rs.11,970-55 x 12 x 16). Therefore, the Petitioners are entitled for Rupees
22,98,345-60 towards loss of dependency due to death of M.Eshwarappa S/o. Venkataramanappa,
which is rounded off Rupees 22,98,346/-.
30. The P.W.1 has stated that, she has spent a sum of Rupees 1,00,000/- towards transportation of
the dead body, funeral expenses and obsequies ceremonies, etc. But, to consider the same, the
Petitioners have only produced Ex.P.12 Ambulance Bill.
31. As per the principles laid down in the decision reported in 2013 ACJ 1403 (Rajesh and Others
V/s Rajbir Singh and Others), loss of consortium to the Petitioner No.1, who is a wife of the
deceased, should be Rupees 1,00,000/-, for loss of care and guidance for minor child should be
Rupees 1,00,000/-, loss of love and affection has to be compensated by awarding Rupees 25,000/-
and funeral expenses should be Rupees 25,000/-. As this Tribunal has already observed that, the
Petitioner No.1 is a wife and the Petitioner No.2 is a minor daughter of the deceased. Hence, the
Petitioner No.1 is entitled for a sum of Rupees 1,00,000/- towards Loss of consortium and the
Petitioner No.2 is entitled for a sum of Rupees 1,00,000/- towards loss of care and guidance for
minor child and all the Petitioners are entitled for a sum of Rupees 25,000/- towards loss of love
and affection and Rupees 25,000/- towards funeral expenses.
